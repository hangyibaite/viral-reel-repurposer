# Short-form content rules (under 90 seconds)

For short-form content, rhythm and pacing dominate. Argument flow matters less. The viewer drops off in milliseconds if the beat slips.

## Length targets

Match the original's category:
- Storytelling / personal-experience reels: 50-55 seconds
- Educational / how-to reels: under 45 seconds
- Tutorial / step-by-step reels: 60-80 seconds
- Hot takes / contrarian reels: 30-45 seconds

If the new topic needs less content, compress. Do not pad.
If the new topic needs more, find expansion room within the original's existing beats. Do not add new beats.

## Pacing rules

- Hook lands within the first 2 seconds. Non-negotiable.
- Rehook #1 lands around 5 seconds.
- Rehook #2 lands around 15 seconds.
- A beat change (rhythm shift, cutscene, energy reset) every 3-7 seconds throughout.
- The hardest-hitting line lands in the FINAL quarter of the runtime.
- The CTA lands in the last 5-10 seconds.

When repurposing: keep each of these beat positions within ±1 second of the original.

## Rhythm patterns to preserve verbatim

If the original uses any of these, preserve them exactly:

- Rule-of-three: "X, Y, Z." Match the three-beat pattern.
- Rule-of-four with intensifier on last: "X. Y. Z. Fucking W." Preserve the intensifier on the last.
- Repetition opener: "It's how we got X. It's how we got Y. It's how we got Z." Preserve the anaphora.
- Repetition closer: "Not X. Not Y. Not Z. It's W." Preserve the rhythm.
- Stepped enumeration: "Step one, X. Step two, Y. Step three, Z." Preserve the numbering cadence.
- Beat with confirmation: "X. Capisce?" / "X. You get it?" / "X. Right?" Preserve the confirmation phrase.
- "Now, you can..." anaphora across consecutive sentences

When the content needs to swap inside one of these patterns, preserve the rhythm and swap only the swappable words. Do not collapse a rule-of-three into a rule-of-two.

## Voice markers to preserve

Even when content changes, keep:
- Cussing for emphasis (if original cusses, the new version cusses in the same places)
- ALL-CAPS for shouting / strong emphasis
- Em-dashes for self-interruption
- Lowercase fillers as written ("rlly", "freakin'", "lil'", "shuddup", "y'know")
- Fake-formal interjections ("Ladies and gentlemen", "Pro tip", "Pro tip for anyone who...")
- Pattern interrupts ("WAIT", "STOP", "[beat]", "(deadpan)")
- Stage directions in brackets ([CUTSCENE: ...], [whisper], [overlay text: ...])

These are voice-coded. Strip any of them and the script reads generic.

## Cutscene placement

If the original has cutscenes / pattern interrupts:
- Note their exact timestamp
- Preserve the slot (a cutscene at 0:15 in the original gets a cutscene at 0:15 in the new version)
- Match the cutscene's TONE (whisper / shouting / mocking / pro tip / vine boom / etc.)
- The content of the cutscene can flex but the function and energy must hold

If the original has no cutscenes, do not add them.

## Length math when content shifts

If the new topic genuinely has fewer steps or beats than the original:
- Compress proportionally across all sections
- Do NOT compress the hook (always 2 seconds, full force)
- Do NOT compress the CTA (always 5-10 seconds)
- Compress the build / explanation sections first
- Last to compress: the payoff line

If the new topic needs more content:
- Expand the build / explanation, not the hook or payoff
- Stay within the original's rhythm pattern even when adding length
- If you can't fit, suggest cutting a sub-point rather than padding

## Common short-form failures

- Compressing the hook to fit a tool name → DON'T. Cut elsewhere.
- Padding the build to hit a target runtime → DON'T. Keep tight.
- Adding rehooks the original didn't have → DON'T. Match the original's rehook count.
- Front-loading the payoff because it's the most exciting line → DON'T. Final quarter.
- Burying the CTA in the middle → DON'T. Last 5-10 seconds.
- Using markdown formatting inside the script (bold, italics via asterisks) → DON'T. Plain text with brackets and CAPS.
